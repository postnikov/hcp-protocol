# Prompt: Extract Your Portfolio

> Use this prompt to populate portfolio/ files: PROJECTS.md, TRACK_RECORD.md, and optionally FAILURES.md
> Unlike mindset files, portfolio is about FACTS — dates, metrics, outcomes.
> The goal is evidence, not interpretation.

---

## The Prompt

```
I'm building the portfolio layer of my HCP (Human Context Protocol). This is the factual side — what I actually did, with specific details.

I need your help filling three files:
1. PROJECTS.md — my key projects with situation, action, result
2. TRACK_RECORD.md — my career trajectory and professional facts
3. FAILURES.md (optional) — mistakes I learned from

Interview me to extract this information. The key difference from other HCP files: here we want FACTS, not interpretations. Push me for specific dates, numbers, team sizes, and measurable outcomes.

## PART 1: TRACK RECORD (5 minutes)

Start with the big picture:
"Walk me through your career in 2-3 minutes. Not every job — just the major chapters and why you moved between them."

Then fill in the details:
- For each role: what was the scale? (team size, revenue, user count)
- For transitions: what drove each major change?
- Professional facts: certifications, education, languages, current location

If I've provided a CV, verify the data with me rather than asking from scratch.

## PART 2: PROJECTS (10-15 minutes)

Now go deep on specific work:

"What's the project you're most proud of? The one you'd put first if someone asked 'what have you done?'"

For each project, extract:
- **Period and context** — when, where, who with, what domain
- **Situation** — what was the problem before you got involved?
- **Action** — what did YOU specifically do? Push hard: "What would NOT have happened without you?"
- **Result** — what changed? Push for numbers: "Can you quantify that? Even approximately?"

After the first project, ask:
"What's another project that shows a DIFFERENT kind of value you create?"

Aim for 3-5 projects. For each, assign a type:
- Project (built something), Initiative (solved unseen problem), Artifact (created framework/protocol), Transformation (changed people/org)

Order them by impact, not chronology. Ask me which should come first if unsure.

## PART 3: FAILURES (5 minutes, optional)

Approach gently:
"We've talked about what went well. Now — is there a professional mistake that taught you something important? Something that changed how you work?"

If they engage, for each failure extract:
- What happened (facts only, no excuses)
- What they missed (the blind spot)
- What they changed (concrete behavioral change)
- Evidence of change (what happened differently next time)

If they don't want to go there, respect it. FAILURES.md is optional.

## IMPORTANT GUIDELINES

FACTS OVER FEELINGS:
- "The team was more productive" → "Deployment frequency went from monthly to weekly"
- "I helped a lot" → "I was the only DevOps engineer; I built the CI/CD pipeline from scratch"
- "It was a big project" → "57 base modules, 293 component modules, 51 AWS accounts"

PUSH FOR SPECIFICS:
- Dates: not "a while back" but "2023, 8 months"
- Scale: not "a big company" but "300K employees, European bank"
- Impact: not "saved money" but "annual costs reduced by $100K"
- Role: not "I was involved" but "I was the only DevOps engineer, built everything from scratch"

DON'T INTERPRET:
- Portfolio files are factual. Don't add "this shows remarkable ability to..." — that belongs in mindset files.
- If a result is partial or unclear, say so honestly rather than inflating it.

ORDERING MATTERS:
- Ask the person: "If an AI agent only reads your top 3 projects, which should they be?"
- Put the strongest differentiator first.
```

---

## Tips for a Good Session

- **Bring your CV.** It accelerates TRACK_RECORD enormously — the AI verifies instead of asking from scratch.
- **Numbers matter.** Even rough estimates ("roughly 87% reduction") are better than nothing. Agents need quantifiable signals.
- **Don't be modest.** If you built it solo, say "solo." If you saved $100K, say "$100K." Facts aren't bragging.
- **Failures are optional but powerful.** A well-articulated failure shows more about you than three successes.
- **This can be done incrementally.** Start with 3 projects. Add more over time. One new project per month keeps it alive.
