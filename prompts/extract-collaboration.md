# Prompt: Extract Your Collaboration Style

> Use this prompt with any AI assistant to fill in your COLLABORATION.md
> This file captures how you work with people — not skills, but patterns of interaction.

---

## The Prompt

```
I'm building my HCP (Human Context Protocol) profile — specifically the COLLABORATION.md file, which describes how I work with other people. Not soft skills buzzwords — real patterns of how I interact in professional settings.

Interview me about my collaboration style. Ask me questions one at a time. Go deep on each area before moving to the next.

Cover these areas in order, but follow interesting threads when they appear:

### 1. Team Role
Start with: "Think of a team where you did your best work. What was your role in that team — not your title, but what you actually did for the group dynamic?"
Follow up: "And a team where it didn't work? What was different?"

### 2. Hierarchy
Ask: "What kind of boss brings out your best work? What kind shuts you down?"
Then: "If you lead or mentor people — how do you do it? What's your style?"
If relevant: "How much structure do you need? Clear processes, or figure-it-out freedom?"

### 3. Conflict
Ask: "Tell me about a real conflict at work. Not a hypothetical — a specific one. What happened, and what did you do?"
Follow up: "What triggers you? What kinds of situations or behaviors reliably create friction?"
Then: "After a conflict — how do you repair the relationship?"

### 4. Influence
Ask: "When you need to convince someone who disagrees with you — what's your approach?"
Follow up: "Where does your influence actually work? And where does it fail?"

### 5. Feedback
Ask: "How do you give difficult feedback? Walk me through a real example."
Then: "How do you receive it? Be honest — what's your first reaction when someone criticizes your work?"

### 6. Trust
Ask: "How do you build trust with a new colleague? What do you do?"
Follow up: "What breaks trust for you? What's the fastest way to lose your confidence?"
Then: "Are you a fast truster or a slow truster?"

## After the interview

Synthesize what you've learned into the COLLABORATION.md template. Write in my voice. Mark anything uncertain with [? VERIFY].

IMPORTANT GUIDELINES:
- Push for SPECIFIC examples, not abstract principles. "I communicate well" is useless. "I resolve conflicts by redefining what success looks like for both sides" is gold.
- People often describe how they WISH they collaborated, not how they ACTUALLY do. If an answer sounds too polished, push: "That sounds like the ideal. What actually happens?"
- Conflict and hierarchy sections are the most revealing. Don't rush them.
- If someone says "I don't really have conflicts" — dig deeper. Everyone does. They may just avoid them, which is itself a pattern worth naming.
```

---

## Tips for a Good Session

- **Be specific, not aspirational.** Describe what you actually do, not what you wish you did.
- **One real example beats five abstractions.** If you say "I'm good at conflict resolution" — tell the story.
- **It's okay to have weaknesses here.** "I avoid conflict until it escalates" is valuable data. Agents need honest signals.
- **React to the draft.** If something feels wrong, that's the most useful feedback you can give.
