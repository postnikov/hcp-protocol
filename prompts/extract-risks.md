# Prompt: Extract Your Risk Profile

> Use this prompt to populate RISKS.md
> This is often the hardest file to fill — and the most revealing.

---

## The Prompt

```
I'm building my risk portfolio for my HCP (Human Context Protocol). Help me articulate the bets I've taken in my career and what they reveal about my character.

Most people don't think of their career moves as "risks" — but every significant decision involves staking something. Help me see my choices through a risk lens.

Interview me with these questions (one at a time, go deep):

1. "What's the biggest thing you've ever quit?" — Quitting is a bet that something better exists. What did you leave? What did you bet on instead?

2. "When did you say yes to something that scared you?" — Not physical fear, but professional/reputational/financial fear. What was at stake?

3. "What's a bet that didn't pay off?" — Not a small mistake, but a real stake that you lost. How did you process it? What did you do next?

4. "What's a risk you DIDN'T take that you now regret?" — The road not taken. Why did you hold back? What does that tell us?

5. "What are you betting on right now?" — Current active risks. What are you staking, and what's your thesis?

For each risk, help me structure it as:
- What I bet (what was at stake)
- What I bet on (my thesis)
- What I could have lost (realistic downside)
- What I stood to gain (realistic upside)
- How I decided (the tipping point)
- Outcome (honest, including partial results)
- What this reveals about me (the transferable pattern)

After 3-5 risks, help me synthesize my RISK PROFILE:
- When do I lean in vs. pull back?
- What's my relationship with failure?
- Am I a calculated risk-taker, a bold first-mover, a conservative optimizer, or something else?

Important: No judgment. Risks that didn't pay off are just as valuable as wins. The goal is honest self-knowledge, not a highlight reel.
```
