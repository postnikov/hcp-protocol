# Prompt: Extract Your Decisions

> Use this prompt to populate DECISIONS.md
> Best done in multiple sessions — one decision per conversation works well.

---

## The Prompt

```
I'm building my decision portfolio for my HCP (Human Context Protocol). I need your help extracting and structuring key decisions from my career.

Interview me about ONE specific decision at a time. Use the SCROE format:
- Situation — what was happening
- Constraints — what limited my options  
- Reasoning — how I evaluated alternatives
- Outcome — what happened
- Extraction — what pattern this reveals

Here's how to interview me:

1. Start by asking: "Tell me about a professional decision you're proud of — OR one you learned the most from."

2. Once I share, go deeper with questions like:
   - "What options did you consider but reject? Why?"
   - "What was the hardest trade-off?"
   - "What information did you wish you had?"
   - "Who pushed back, and how did you handle it?"
   - "Knowing what you know now, what would you change?"

3. After the deep dive, write it up in the SCROE format. Pay special attention to the EXTRACTION — the transferable pattern that makes this decision relevant regardless of industry or tech stack.

4. Then ask: "What's ANOTHER decision that shows a DIFFERENT side of how you think?" This ensures variety in the portfolio.

Important guidelines:
- Dig for the REASONING, not just the story. I can tell you what happened — you need to help me articulate WHY I chose what I chose.
- Include at least one decision that didn't work out. Frame it as learning, not failure.
- Strip out technology names where possible — focus on the pattern. "Chose a statically typed language for a critical system" ages better than "chose Go over Node.js."
- Keep the human element. If emotions played a role in the decision, include that — it's data, not weakness.
```

---

## Decision Domains to Cover

Try to build a portfolio that spans multiple domains:

- [ ] A **technical architecture** decision
- [ ] A **people/team** decision (hiring, firing, reorganizing)
- [ ] A **product/strategy** decision
- [ ] A **personal career** decision (quitting, pivoting, saying no)
- [ ] A **decision under extreme uncertainty**
- [ ] A **decision that failed** and what you learned

5-8 well-described decisions create a powerful profile.
