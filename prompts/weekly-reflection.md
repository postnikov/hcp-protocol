# Prompt: Weekly HCP Reflection

> Run this every Friday (or whenever your week ends).
> Takes 10-15 minutes. Keeps your HCP alive.

---

## The Prompt

```
It's time for my weekly HCP reflection. Ask me these questions one at a time. Listen for material that should update my DECISIONS.md, CAPABILITIES.md, or RISKS.md.

1. "What was the hardest decision you made this week?"
   → If significant, structure it as a DECISIONS.md entry.

2. "What did you learn or get better at?"
   → If it's a new capability or growth in an existing one, note it for CAPABILITIES.md.

3. "Did you take any risks or make any bets this week?"
   → Even small ones count. Career conversations, project proposals, saying no to something safe.

4. "What would you do differently if you could redo this week?"
   → This often surfaces decision patterns worth documenting.

5. "Is there anything you did this week that surprised you about yourself?"
   → These are signals that IDENTITY.md might need updating.

After my answers, tell me:
- Which HCP files should be updated
- Draft the specific additions/changes
- Flag if you notice any patterns emerging across weeks

Keep it conversational, not bureaucratic. This should feel like a useful reflection, not a compliance exercise.
```

---

## Monthly Expansion (do once a month instead of weekly)

```
Let's do a monthly HCP review. Look at my updates from the past 4 weeks and help me:

1. **Pattern check** — Are there recurring themes in my weekly decisions? What's the meta-pattern?
2. **Capability evolution** — Have any capabilities shifted in autonomy level? Am I getting better at something specific?
3. **Risk calibration** — Am I taking more or fewer risks than last month? Is that intentional?
4. **Identity drift** — Based on this month's data, is my IDENTITY.md still accurate? Or am I becoming someone slightly different?

Synthesize into a brief monthly summary and suggest specific edits to my HCP files.
```
