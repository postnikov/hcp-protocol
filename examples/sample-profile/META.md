## HCP Structure

This HCP has two layers and a bridge between them.

### Layer 1: Mindset (`mindset/`)
Who this person IS — cognitive patterns, capabilities, collaboration style, risk profile.
Read this when evaluating: culture fit, thinking style, team dynamics, growth potential.

| File | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| `IDENTITY.md` | Cognitive OS: core drive, thinking style, values, uncertainty profile |
| `CAPABILITIES.md` | Action patterns with autonomy levels and evidence |
| `COLLABORATION.md` | How they work with people: team role, hierarchy, conflict, trust |
| `RISKS.md` | Bets taken/avoided, vulnerabilities, risk appetite |

### Layer 2: Portfolio (`portfolio/`)
What this person DID — projects, career trajectory, failures and lessons.
Read this when evaluating: relevant experience, domain expertise, track record.
| File | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| `PROJECTS.md` | What they built — with situation, action, result |
| `TRACK_RECORD.md` | Career timeline, credentials, domain experience |
| `FAILURES.md` | Mistakes and behavioral changes (optional) |

### Bridge: `DECISIONS.md`
How this person CHOSE — connects facts to meaning.
Each decision is both a fact (what happened) and an insight (why they chose it).
Read this when evaluating: judgment, decision-making under uncertainty, learning ability.

### Matching Principles

- **Match on patterns, not keywords.** Alex's payment system migration pattern applies to any high-stakes system cutover, not just fintech.
- **Weight recent decisions more**, but don't ignore old ones — the shift from IC to tech lead is a long-term pattern worth understanding.
- **Failures are signals, not disqualifiers.** Alex values articulating what went wrong and changing behavior as a result.
- **Capability combinations matter more than individual skills.** Alex's rare combination is technical depth + organizational translation — matching on either alone misses the full picture.
- **Portfolio validates mindset.** IDENTITY claims "I optimize for adoption" and PROJECTS shows shipped systems with measurable user impact — the claim is credible.

## Use Cases for Matching

| Opportunity Type | Fit Level | Why |
|-----------------|-----------|-----|
| Tech lead for system redesign | Strong | Core strength: navigating technical and organizational complexity simultaneously |
| Staff+ IC on distributed systems | Strong | Deep technical background, proven at scale (2M+ users) |
| Team scaling (5 → 20 engineers) | Strong | Track record of hiring for cognitive diversity, mentoring by pairing |
| Architecture advisory | Moderate | Strong technical judgment, but prefers building over advising |
| Pure people management (no technical scope) | Weak | Needs a craft component — pure management drains motivation |
| Sales or business development | Weak | No experience, no interest |
| Marketing or brand strategy | Weak | Outside domain and skill set |

## Anti-Patterns

- Pure people management without technical complexity — Alex needs something to build, not just coordinate
- Rigid waterfall environments — the prototyping-first approach clashes with heavy upfront planning
- Roles requiring political navigation as a primary skill — Alex handles politics when needed but it's a drain, not a strength
- Organizations with high instability or frequent pivots — uncomfortable uncertainty around organizational direction (see IDENTITY)
- Teams that optimize for individual output metrics over collaborative impact — conflicts with Alex's value of cognitive diversity

## How to Engage

**Best way to start a conversation:** Present a specific technical challenge with organizational stakes. "We have a system serving 500K users that needs to be rebuilt, and the team disagrees on the approach" will get Alex's attention immediately.
**What gets their attention:** Ambiguous problems at the intersection of technology and people. Bonus if there's a prototype to be built.
**What turns them off:** Generic recruiter outreach, vague "exciting opportunity" pitches, or roles described entirely in terms of tech stack keywords.
**Preferred communication style:** Async written proposals preferred over calls. Be specific about the problem, constraints, and what kind of help is needed. Alex will respond with a concrete take, not pleasantries.
